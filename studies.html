Introduction

The vampire subculture is more than aesthetics: Psychological and sociological research views it as an alternative identity form, helping some manage anxiety or dissociation. But studies warn of vulnerabilities (e.g., teens in crisis). This 2025 panorama, based on academic reviews, balances benefits and precautions.

Psychological Perspectives

  • Benefits: "Psychic vampirism" as metaphor for extreme empathy; therapies via communities (e.g., pranic energy for depression).
  • Risks: Links to dissociative disorders (Renfield syndrome); 10-15% of members report isolation (BU study 2023/2025).

Sociological Perspectives

  • Communities: Networks like "otherkin" foster inclusion; ethnographic analysis of NOLA (Mellins, 2011/2025 update).
  • Drifts: "Pay-cults" exploit financially vulnerable; global cases (Africa: rumors as social control).
