Introduction

In Africa and Oceania, the vampire subculture merges indigenous folklore (e.g., Ghanaian asanbosam) with Western influences, often through colonial rumors or urban goth. Less organized than US "courts" (~2,000-4,000 members globally estimated), these communities favor online forums and private meetups. In 2025, focus on Australia/New Zealand; in Africa, vigilance on religious conflicts. Note: Few public events, but growth via video games and social media.

History and Context

  • Africa: Colonial "vampire" rumors (1920s-1950s) as metaphors for exploitation (e.g., South African mines). Today: Hybrids with voodoo or evangelical Christianity.
  • Oceania: Imported goth 1990s; Maori folklore (bloodthirsty spirits) in NZ. 2025: VEKN newsletter highlights active LARP.
